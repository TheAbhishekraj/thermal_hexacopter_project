## Slide 3: Why Thermal Imaging?
**Content:**
- **Header:** "The Science: Plants 'Sweat' Less When Sick"
- **Healthy Plant Physiology:**
  - Transpiration: Evaporative cooling through stomata
  - Leaf temperature: 2-5¬∞C cooler than ambient air
  - Energy balance: $R_n = \lambda E + H + G$
- **Diseased Plant Physiology:**
  - Fungal toxins ‚Üí Stomatal closure ‚Üí 40-60% transpiration reduction
  - Vascular blockage ‚Üí Water stress ‚Üí 1-2¬∞C elevation
  - Metabolic heat ‚Üí Active fungal respiration ‚Üí 0.5-1.5¬∞C elevation
  - **Result:** Diseased tissue 2-4¬∞C warmer (detectable 3-5 days before visual symptoms)
- **Visual:** Diagram showing stomata (open vs. closed) with heat arrows

**Speaker Notes (90 seconds):**
- Explain LWIR spectrum (7.5-14 Œºm) vs. visible light
- Discuss emissivity correction for maize leaves (Œµ ‚âà 0.95-0.98)
- Mention Seek Thermal CompactPRO: 0.1¬∞C resolution, 640√ó480 pixels

## Anticipated Questions & Responses

**Q1: "Why not use satellite imagery instead of drones?"**
- **A:** Satellite resolution (10-30m/pixel) insufficient for 0.4 hectare plots; revisit time (5-16 days) too slow for disease progression; cloud cover blocks optical/thermal sensors 40-60% of monsoon season

**Q2: "How do you handle false positives from soil temperature variation?"**
- **A:** Vegetation masking (NDVI > 0.3) excludes bare soil; temporal filtering (compare multiple flights); spatial clustering (disease spreads radially, not randomly)

**Q3: "What about battery safety (LiPo fire risk)?"**
- **A:** LiPo safe bags for storage/charging; voltage monitoring (land at 3.7V/cell); temperature sensors (abort if >60¬∞C); farmer training on battery handling

**Q4: "Can farmers afford ‚Çπ1.25 lakh?"**
- **A:** Drone-as-a-service model: 10-20 farmer cooperatives share costs (‚Çπ6,250-12,500 per farmer); government subsidies (50% under PM-KISAN scheme); microfinance options

**Q5: "How does this compare to handheld thermal cameras?"**
- **A:** Handheld requires manual field walking (2-3 hours/hectare); drone covers 4 hectares/hour (8√ó faster); aerial perspective enables spatial disease mapping; autonomous operation frees farmer time
